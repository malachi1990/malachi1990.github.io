## Musing 130

One of the things I despise is political theater. For a  lot of reasons - at the most basic, it wastes
everyone's time, and time is precious. On top of this, it distracts from the real solutions that will
help the gov't do its duties.

But most crucially, such theater is an affront to God because it is a dereliction of duty and oftentimes
necessitates some level of dishonesty.

While we often look at dishonesty as not that big a deal, remember the scope of a politician's lies. Their
actions have impact on thousands or millions of lives depending on what level of gov't they are in. And
so we must not be afraid to hold them to account, even over small things.

But I must also acknowledge a sad reality. Because of the degradation of our culture, the dog and pony 
show is how people become aware of real issues and/or keep people in office because we are a people motivated
not by truth (especially ugly ones) but by emotional framing. Like immature children, we have to be poked and 
prodded to do even the basics, and then only work on things that matter when 'our' guy treats us as the children
we are and riles up the emotions.

May we see this reality repent, and instead be motivated to honor God in truthfulness and a proactive desire
to see sin be replaced with justice and righteousness.
